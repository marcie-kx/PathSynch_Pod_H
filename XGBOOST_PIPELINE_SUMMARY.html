1) Data Processing Flow

1

Raw CSV load and cleanup

Transaction_Date was converted to datetime and invalid dates were removed before preparing the daily time series.

2

Daily aggregation

Revenue was aggregated with sum, Clicks with sum, and Conversion_Rate with mean to build the daily target series.

3

Feature matrix generation

A supervised table was created using calendar, lag, rolling statistics, ad metric lags, and weekday-average features.

4

Leakage removal

Ad features were restricted to previous-day and lag values only to prevent future information leakage.

3) Features Used

The Revenue model used the following feature groups.

  • Day of week, day of month, month, quarter, and week of year
  • Weekend, month start/end, and holiday flags
  • Days until holiday, month end, and quarter end
  • Black Friday, Cyber Monday, New Year, Thanksgiving, and Christmas period flags
  • trend_idx, same_dow_mean_8
  • lag_1, lag_2, lag_3, lag_7, lag_14, lag_21, lag_28, lag_35, lag_56
  • roll_mean_3/7/14/28
  • roll_std_3/7/14/28
  • roll_min_3/7/14/28
  • roll_max_3/7/14/28
  • Ad_Spend_prev_day, Ad_Spend_lag_7/14
  • Ad_CTR_prev_day, Ad_CTR_lag_7/14
  • Ad_CPC_prev_day, Ad_CPC_lag_7/14
  • lag_1_minus_lag_7, lag_7_minus_lag_28
  • roll_mean_7_minus_28

5) Modeling Approach

Final choice: Single Revenue XGBoost model

The operating choice is the single Revenue model and all delivery scripts are aligned to this path.

The single model is the best of the tested options, and the recursive evaluation now clears the 80% target.

6) Deliverables

  • Backtest: 30-day rolling CV with 4 folds
  • Forecast: next 30-day prediction CSV
  • Confidence interval: 85% CI using residual quantiles
Operational note: Revenue is stable under the current setup, but Clicks and Conversion_Rate still need additional features or a revised problem definition.
